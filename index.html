LGBT Elders Are Worried About Adequate Support As They Age

by K. Dominic McKenzie & Allie Weintraub

(tk) (tk)

A report by AARP, the nation’s largest non-profit dedicated to healthy livelihoods for the elderly in the US, found that many LGBT seniors have an interest in LGBT-welcoming housing developments for older adults. In fact, 91 per cent of the respondents agreed. This news comes as construction on New York City’s first senior apartments with services tailored to the needs of LGBT seniors in the city, is ongoing. The developments, Crotona Senior Residences in the Bronx and Ingersoll Senior Residences in Brooklyn, are part of the efforts of the National LGBT Elder Housing Initiative, a project by the group Services and Advocacy for GLBT Elders (SAGE). Interestingly, 73 per cent of respondents said they do not have access to LGBT-specific senior services and just over half said they fear that discrimination or prejudice will impact quality of the care they need to receive as they age.
